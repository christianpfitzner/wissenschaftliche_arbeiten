abstract: |
  A group of Crazyflie micro-drones is now available at the AerodrOHM and can be programmed
  for coordinated formation flights. The goal of this thesis is to implement a demonstration
  in the style of drone light painting: the drones execute predefined flight patterns to
  generate spatial light images that can be captured photographically using long-exposure
  photography. The Bitcraze Lighthouse Painting project serves as thematic orientation.

  In the first step, suitable flight paths are to be designed, covering both simple geometric
  shapes and more complex patterns such as lettering or logos. The trajectories must be
  planned so that collisions between drones are reliably avoided and temporal synchronisation
  of flight movements is ensured, while accounting for the limited flight time of the micro-drones.

  Building on trajectory planning, the coordinated control of multiple drones in the swarm
  is to be realised, investigating suitable communication and synchronisation approaches
  and implementing a central or distributed control architecture. Practical testing takes
  place in the AerodrOHM flight space, where the resulting light images will be photographically documented.

## Work Packages

- Familiarisation with the drone platform and its programming interface
- Design and implementation of flight paths for various light patterns
- Coordination and synchronisation of multiple drones in the swarm
- Experimental testing and photographic documentation of the light images

## Requirements

- Good programming skills in Python
- Interest in autonomous flight and multi-robot systems
- Basic understanding of coordinate systems and spatial geometry

This topic can be completed as a **project, bachelor's or master's thesis** subject to agreement.
