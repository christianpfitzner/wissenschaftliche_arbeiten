abstract: |
  The TTZ Nürnberger Land is acquiring a Reachy Mini from Pollen Robotics — a compact,
  open-source desktop robot specifically designed for exploring human-robot interaction (HRI).
  With six degrees of freedom in its head, a wide-angle camera, four microphones, an integrated
  speaker, and animated antennas, the Reachy Mini provides an expressive platform for intuitive
  and engaging interaction concepts.

  The goal of this thesis is to commission the robot at TTZ and develop one or more HMI
  applications that showcase the system's potential in the context of applied research and
  technology demonstration. The focus is on creating natural and engaging forms of interaction —
  for example through speech, gaze, gesture, or simple emotional expressions of the robot.

  The work begins with an analysis of the available software interfaces and a complete setup
  of the robot. A concrete application scenario is then selected, prototypically implemented,
  and systematically evaluated. Possible scenarios include a visitor information and greeting
  system for the TTZ, an interactive demonstration system for technology presentations, or a
  research setup for studying interaction behaviour.

  The open software architecture of the Reachy Mini — based on Python, a web-based dashboard,
  and integration with Hugging Face Spaces and modern AI models — enables a wide range of
  implementation options and is particularly well-suited for experimental and creative
  approaches in HRI research.

## Motivation

The Reachy Mini is one of the most accessible open-source platforms for exploring
human-robot interaction. Its compact form factor, expressive head movement, and multimodal
sensing (camera, microphones, speaker, antennas) make it ideal for studies and demonstrations
where intuitive human-machine communication is at the forefront.

At TTZ Nürnberger Land, it is intended to serve as a lively research and demonstration
platform — for technology presentations, visitor interaction, and experimental HRI research.

## Work Packages

**Commissioning**
- Assembly and setup of the hardware (Reachy Mini Wireless)
- Installation and configuration of the software environment (Python, web dashboard)
- Exploration of available APIs, Hugging Face Spaces, and development interfaces

**Application Development**
- Selection and elaboration of a concrete HMI application scenario
- Implementation of the interaction logic (speech, gesture, facial expression, camera feedback)
- Integration of AI components (e.g. speech recognition, face detection)

**Evaluation & Documentation**
- User study or structured evaluation of the developed application
- Documentation of the system as a basis for follow-up projects at TTZ

## Requirements

- Basic knowledge of Python
- Interest in human-robot interaction, AI, or service robotics
- Creativity in designing interaction scenarios
- Ideally, first experience with embedded systems, Raspberry Pi, or ROS

Depending on the focus and scope, the topic can be carried out as a **project thesis, bachelor's thesis, or master's thesis**.
